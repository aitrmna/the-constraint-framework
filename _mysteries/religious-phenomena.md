What happens when we examine these through constraint stacking rather than theology?

## CASE 1: FATIMA (1917)

### What Happened

October 13, 1917. Fatima, Portugal. 70,000 people gathered after three children predicted the Virgin Mary would perform a miracle.

**What witnesses reported:**
- The sun "danced," spun, zigzagged across the sky
- Emitted colored light (red, blue, yellow)
- Seemed to plunge toward Earth
- Lasted ~10 minutes
- Wet ground and clothes dried instantly afterward
- Seen from miles away (rules out localized illusion)

**The witnesses:**
- Believers and skeptics both present
- Anti-clerical newspaper reporters documented it
- 70,000+ witnesses (not easily dismissed)

---

### Constraint Analysis: Fatima

**CLAIM TESTED:** Fatima was mass hallucination

**CONSTRAINT 1: Scale**
70,000 people don't hallucinate the same thing simultaneously. Mass hallucination of this specificity has no precedent.

**CONSTRAINT 2: Skeptics Saw It**
Anti-religious reporters present specifically to debunk saw the phenomenon and reported it.

**CONSTRAINT 3: Distant Observation**
Seen from villages miles away by people who didn't know the prediction. Rules out suggestion.

**CONSTRAINT 4: Physical Effects**
Wet ground and clothes dried during/after event. Physical, not just visual.

**ELIMINATES:** Mass hallucination, suggestion, purely psychological

---

**CLAIM TESTED:** Fatima was astronomical event (sun actually moved)

**CONSTRAINT 1:** The sun didn't actually move. Observatories worldwide recorded nothing unusual.

**ELIMINATES:** Literal astronomical phenomenon

---

**What Survives:**

Something happened in the sky that 70,000 people witnessed. It wasn't the literal sun. It wasn't mass hallucination.

**The interesting question:** Was this a UAP event interpreted through 1917 Catholic framework?

**The pattern matches:**
- Aerial phenomenon
- Multiple witnesses
- Physical effects
- High strangeness
- Interpreted through local cultural framework (religious in 1917 Portugal)

## CASE 2: STIGMATA

### What It Is

Wounds appearing on body corresponding to Christ's crucifixion wounds — hands/wrists, feet, side, head (crown of thorns).

**Notable cases:**
- Padre Pio (1918-1968) — wounds for 50 years, bled daily
- Therese Neumann (1926-1962) — bled every Friday
- Contemporary cases documented

---

### Constraint Analysis: Stigmata

**CLAIM TESTED:** Stigmata is self-inflicted or fraud

**CONSTRAINT 1: Padre Pio's Wounds**
Examined by doctors repeatedly over 50 years. Wounds never healed, never infected, bled fresh. Self-infliction doesn't explain 50 years without healing or infection.

**CONSTRAINT 2: Therese Neumann**
Under observation, wounds appeared during religious ecstasy states without physical self-harm. Documented by physicians.

**CONSTRAINT 3: The Pattern**
Wounds correspond to crucifixion BUT vary — some have hand wounds, some wrist wounds, matching the stigmatic's BELIEF about where Christ was wounded.

**What this means:** Wounds match internal image, not historical accuracy. Mind is generating the physical marks.

**ELIMINATES:** Simple fraud (some cases documented under observation)

---

**What Survives:**

In some cases, the mind appears to generate physical wounds corresponding to intensely held beliefs. This is extreme psychosomatic effect — mind affecting body dramatically.

**Connection to our findings:** Mental causation is real (materialism analysis, placebo). Stigmata is an extreme case: belief → physical wounds.

If consciousness is fundamental, mind affecting body isn't mysterious — it's expected.

## CASE 3: MIRACULOUS HEALINGS

### The Lourdes Medical Bureau

The Catholic Church investigates claimed miraculous healings at Lourdes rigorously:
- Must have prior medical documentation of illness
- Must be examined after by medical bureau
- Must be organic illness (not psychosomatic conditions)
- Must be instantaneous or near-instantaneous
- Must be complete
- Must be permanent

**Results:**
- ~7,000 claimed cures
- ~70 declared "miraculous" (meeting all criteria)
- Many more deemed "remarkable" but not meeting all criteria

**Examples:**
- Cancers disappearing instantly
- Multiple sclerosis reversing
- Documented blindness restored

---

### Constraint Analysis: Healings

**CLAIM TESTED:** All healings are misdiagnosis or fraud

**CONSTRAINT 1: Prior Documentation**
Cases require prior medical records. Misdiagnosis possible in some, but across 70 declared cases spanning decades?

**CONSTRAINT 2: Organic Illnesses**
The bureau explicitly excludes conditions that could be psychosomatic. Cancers aren't psychosomatic.

**CONSTRAINT 3: The Spontaneous Remission Rate**
Some cancers do spontaneously remit (~1 in 100,000 for some types). But instantaneous? At the moment of pilgrimage? The coincidence strains.

**What survives:** Some healings appear genuine and unexplained by conventional medicine.

---

**What This Means:**

Something is happening at Lourdes that produces real physical healings in some people. The mechanism is unknown.

**Connection to our findings:** If consciousness is fundamental, and belief can create wounds (stigmata) and affect healing (placebo), then belief-mediated dramatic healing becomes less impossible.

The healings are rare. Most pilgrims aren't healed. But some are. Why those particular ones?
