240-page medieval book in unknown script, illustrated with strange plants, astronomical diagrams, naked women in tubes. Carbon-dated 1404-1438.

No one has deciphered it. Not medieval scholars. Not WWII codebreakers. Not modern AI.

**The test:** What must be true for each explanation? Does reality match?

---

## Competing Hypotheses

1. Random gibberish (elaborate hoax)
2. Simple cipher/encoding
3. Modern forgery
4. Real unknown language or system

---

## H1: Random Gibberish Hoax

**What must be true:**
- Random letter combinations
- No statistical language patterns
- Word distribution random
- Inconsistent vocabulary

**Reality check:**

**Random combinations?** → ✗ CRITICAL FAIL
- Word length distribution follows natural language patterns
- Zipf's Law compliance (word frequency matches real languages)
- Consistent "vocabulary" of ~35,000 word tokens
- Letters combine according to consistent rules
- **Statistical fingerprint of real language, not gibberish**

**No patterns?** → ✗ CRITICAL FAIL
- Word patterns: Some words only in certain sections
- Section-specific vocabulary (like real reference book)
- Consistent grammar-like structure

**Random distribution?** → ✗ CRITICAL FAIL
- Follows linguistic probability distributions
- Creating fake language with proper statistics requires understanding linguistics that didn't exist in 1400s

**Score:** 0/3 passes, 3 critical fails
**Result:** Dead. Statistical tests eliminate random hoax.

## H2: Simple Cipher/Encoding

**What must be true:**
- Pattern matches known medieval ciphers

**Reality check:**

**Matches known ciphers?** → ✗ CRITICAL FAIL
- Doesn't match any known medieval cipher structure
- Medieval ciphers show patterns (substitution leaves traces)
- Voynichese doesn't match these patterns

**Substitution detectable?** → ✗ CRITICAL FAIL
- Too many unique "words" for simple substitution
- Frequency analysis fails
- Every known cipher technique fails

**Partial success?** → ✗ CRITICAL FAIL
- WWII codebreakers (cracked Enigma) failed
- NSA cryptographers failed
- Computer analysis failed
- AI language models failed
- Hundreds of claimed "solutions" - none verified

**Matches decoded?** → ✗ FAIL
- Word structure doesn't match Latin, Greek, or known medieval languages

**Score:** 0/4 passes, 3 critical + 1 fail
**Result:** Dead. Not a simple cipher.

## H3: Modern Forgery

**What must be true:**
- Vellum recently produced
- Ink modern composition
- Provenance suspicious or recent

**Reality check:**

**Recent vellum?** → ✗ CRITICAL FAIL
- Vellum carbon-dated 1404-1438
- Physical pages are 600 years old
- Cannot fake carbon dating

**Modern ink?** → ✗ FAIL
- Ink consistent with period
- Chemical analysis shows age

**Recent provenance?** → ✗ FAIL
- First confirmed owner: Georg Baresch (1600s) - was puzzled by it
- Passed to Athanasius Kircher, then Jesuits, then Wilfrid Voynich (1912)
- 400+ year documented history

**Score:** 0/3 passes, 1 critical + 2 fails
**Result:** Dead. Genuinely medieval.
