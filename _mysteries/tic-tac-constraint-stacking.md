## The Incident (Brief)

November 2004, USS Nimitz carrier group off San Diego. For two weeks, radar tracked objects dropping from 80,000 feet to sea level in seconds. Commander David Fravor visually encountered a 40-foot white object with no wings, no exhaust. It mirrored his movements, then accelerated instantaneously — reappearing 60 miles away at his classified destination. Multiple radar systems and FLIR video recorded it. Pentagon confirmed.

### CONSTRAINT 1 (MOST DAMNING): The Physics

**The Evidence:**
- Object dropped from 80,000 feet to sea level in seconds
- Object accelerated from hover to "gone" instantaneously
- Object traveled 60 miles to Fravor's CAP point in under a minute
- No sonic boom despite hypersonic apparent speed

**Calculated Forces:**
- Minimum: 175g (if generous assumptions about time)
- Likely: 1,000-5,000g (based on witness descriptions)
- No known material survives 175g sustained
- No human survives more than ~10g briefly
- No known propulsion produces this acceleration without massive exhaust

**ELIMINATES:**
- ❌ All known human aircraft (max ~15g, with pilot blackout)
- ❌ All known missiles (max ~40g)
- ❌ All known drones (structural limits far below 100g)
- ❌ Any piloted craft (human body limit)
- ❌ Any conventional propulsion (would produce heat, exhaust, sonic boom)

**SOLUTION SPACE REMAINING:**
1. Sensor error (all systems showed same impossible physics)
2. Natural phenomenon (somehow matching physics signature)
3. Technology using unknown physics
4. Non-human technology

**DAMNING POWER: NUCLEAR** — Eliminates all known human technology in one stroke.

## ANOMALIES (Must Fit Within Solution Space)

**Anomaly 1:** "Why would non-human intelligence be interested in a carrier group?"

**Fits within solution:** Reconnaissance. Carrier groups are concentrations of advanced human military technology. Interest in observing is plausible for any intelligence monitoring Earth.

**Anomaly 2:** "Why no communication or aggression?"

**Fits within solution:** Observation mission. Or communication in ways we don't recognize. Or indifference. Nothing about "non-human" requires contact.

**Anomaly 3:** "Why let itself be seen?"

**Fits within solution:** Perhaps indifferent to being observed. Perhaps intended to be seen. Perhaps our detection capability exceeded expectations. We can't assume non-human motivations match human operational security.

**All anomalies fit within "non-human technology" solution. None break the constraint network.**
